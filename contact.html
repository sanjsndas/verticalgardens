Frequently Asked Questions

What's the best vertical garden system for beginners? +
We recommend starting with a simple tower garden or wall-mounted pocket system. These are affordable, easy to set up, and perfect for learning the basics. Begin with herbs and leafy greens - they're forgiving and provide quick results that build confidence.
How much space do I need for a vertical garden? +
You can start with as little as 2 square feet! A basic tower system fits in a 2x2 foot space, while wall-mounted systems only need vertical wall space. Even a small balcony or patio corner can accommodate a productive vertical garden.
What plants work best in vertical gardens? +
Leafy greens (lettuce, spinach, kale), herbs (basil, cilantro, parsley), and strawberries are excellent choices. These plants have shallow root systems and adapt well to container growing. Avoid large plants like full-size tomatoes unless you have a robust support system.
How often do I need to water my vertical garden? +
Vertical gardens typically need daily watering, especially in hot weather. The top containers dry out fastest. Check soil moisture daily and water when the top inch feels dry. Consider drip irrigation systems for consistent watering.
Can I grow vegetables indoors vertically? +
Absolutely! With proper LED grow lights, you can grow fresh vegetables year-round indoors. Focus on leafy greens, herbs, and compact varieties. You'll need lights that provide at least 30-50 watts per square foot for most vegetables.
